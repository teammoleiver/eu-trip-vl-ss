Exit day — leaving Belarus the smart way

Since 1 Jan 2026, passenger cars register remotely in the electronic queue for all Belarus→EU crossings — you get notified when to drive to the checkpoint instead of camping in a line.

📲 E-queue (do this from Minsk)

  • Register the car at belarusborder.by — remote registration for passenger cars, fee ~22.5 BYN (≈ €7), payable via ERIP/bank.
  • Brest's waiting area holds 235 cars with displays calling plates forward; you can monitor the live count on gpk.gov.by.
  • A paid fixed-date reservation exists but costs ~697 BYN (~€200) — skip it; plain registration + midweek timing is enough.
  • Best slots: weekday mornings. Avoid Friday/Sunday evenings.

🧾 Before the border

  • Pay all speeding fines — unpaid ones surface at exit control and can hold you for hours.
  • Return the BelToll unit → deposit back (~€25).
  • Keep the temporary-import customs paper from entry — it's checked on the way out.
  • EU duty-free limits going back in: alcohol/tobacco strictly enforced at Terespol.
  • Fill the tank — last cheap fuel before the EU.

🧭 Which border out?

  • Brest → Terespol (recommended) — open for all passenger traffic, straight onto your known route to Warsaw.
  • Lithuania (Kamenny Log → Medininkai) and Latvia (Grigorovshchina → Pāternieki): possible but currently seeing delays up to several days — only worth it if live news says otherwise that week.
  • Check the live panels below + gpk.gov.by outbound queue the day before.

🛂 Customs back into the EU: from a non-EU country, meat, milk and dairy are banned and honey/other animal products are limited — so favour chocolate, sweets, linen, crafts and cosmetics. Alcohol duty-free per adult: 1 L of spirits over 22% (or 2 L up to 22%) plus 4 L wine; tobacco 200 cigarettes; other goods up to about €430 per adult (€150 for under-15s).
