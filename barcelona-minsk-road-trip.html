🛂 Customs into Belarus: Belarus is in the EAEU customs union, and EU counter-sanctions plus veterinary rules restrict meat and dairy imports — jamón and cheese can be questioned, so keep them sealed and clearly for personal use. Alcohol is roughly 3 L per adult duty-free and tobacco is limited; declare anything above the allowances. When in doubt, favour sweets, oil, tinned goods and non-edible keepsakes.

Fuel — prices, where to fill & cost ⛽

The car is a 2021 Toyota Corolla 1.8 Hybrid Touring Sports with a roof box. Loaded, at a steady 110–120 km/h, budget about 6.0 L/100 km of petrol E95 (it sips far less unladen, but the roof box and motorway speed add up). Tank ≈ 43 L ≈ ~700 km per fill. Golden rule: fill up in the cheap countries — Spain, Germany, Czechia, Poland — and just roll through France, whose motorway aires are the priciest fuel on the whole route.

Where to actually fill the tank

✅ Five full-tank stops

  • Barcelona — start full (Repsol / Moeve / Plenoil, ~€1.44).
  • La Jonquera, ES — top up at GALP/Moeve; last Spanish prices before France.
  • Karlsruhe / Pforzheim, DE — JET/discount; covers you into Czechia.
  • Plzeň, CZ — Benzina / MOL / EuroOil, past the border markup.
  • Wrocław, PL — Orlen / Lotos ~€1.39; the cheapest fill of the trip.

⚠️ Where NOT to buy

  • French autoroute aires — up to €2.50/L. A Leclerc/Intermarché just off the exit is ~15–20% cheaper.
  • Rozvadov / Waidhaus (DE–CZ border) — inflated "tourist trap"; drive 20–30 km into Czechia first.
  • Any station right at a border — nearly always marked up.

Two ways to sleep cheap between Barcelona and Warsaw: free & budget van/camp spots right off the motorway (park4night), and cheap whole Airbnb flats & gîtes in villages just off the exits. Every card links straight to the source and to Google Maps — pick whichever suits each night.

⚠️ Tent rule: free aires and car parks are for sleeping inside the vehicle only. In Germany and France, pitching a tent outside a campsite is prohibited and can be fined. On-board rules of the road

The three checklists that keep the trip smooth: baby, roof box, and pacing.

👶 Baby & family

  • Stop every 60–90 min for feeding, nappy changes and a short walk out of the car seat.
  • Changing mat, wipes, spare outfits and toys within arm's reach in the cabin.
  • Drive during the normal nap window (e.g. after lunch) when possible.
  • Cooler bag with formula, snacks and water in the cabin — never left in a hot car.
  • Recheck the car-seat anchors after each overnight stop.
  • Basic first-aid kit: thermometer, infant paracetamol, rehydration sachets.
  • If sleeping in the car, pick a manned, well-lit service area — check park4night.com first.

📦 Roof box (Corolla)

  • Keep total roof load under the rack's and the car's roof limits (often ~50–75 kg incl. the box).
  • Light, bulky items up top; heavy or fragile items stay in the cabin or boot.
  • Cap speed at 100–120 km/h — even on the autobahn — for stability and fuel economy.
  • Re-tighten straps every morning and after each fuel stop.
  • Open net or basket? Waterproof cargo bag or tarp underneath.

🔁 Drivers & pacing

  • Two drivers: swap at every rest stop, roughly every 1–1.5 h.
  • No one drives more than ~90 min at a stretch with the baby aboard.
  • Day 4 is the longest (~6 h 45 m) — start at 07:00 and take the extra-long break in Wrocław.

The Belarus leg — what changes at the border

From the Brest barrier onward, the EU rulebook stops. These are the must-knows for taking your Spanish-registered Corolla into Belarus.

📄 Car papers & insurance

  • Green Card is NOT valid — Belarus was suspended from the Green Card system in 2023. Buy border insurance at Brest (~€25–35 / 15 days), cash.
  • BelToll: foreign cars under 3.5 t must register and carry the on-board unit (deposit ~€25) — sold at the border. Return it when leaving to get the deposit back.
  • Bring the original Permiso de Circulación + Ficha Técnica (customs temporary-import declaration needs them) and the Aistren purchase invoice — the car changed owners only weeks ago.
  • International Driving Permit recommended alongside the Spanish licence.

💶 Money, SIM & rules

  • EU roaming ends at the border — buy an A1 or MTS SIM (passport needed) in Brest or Kobryn. Download offline maps before crossing.
  • EU-issued cards are unreliable in Belarus — carry cash. Note EU sanctions restrict exporting euro banknotes to Belarus beyond personal use; USD is a safe alternative. Exchange to BYN locally.
  • Mandatory in the car in Belarus: fire extinguisher, first aid kit, warning triangle.
  • Do NOT bring drones or radio scanners; never photograph border posts. Watch speed — cameras are everywhere on the M1.
  • Staying over 10 days at a private address? Register your stay.

🗓️ Before 16 July — critical

  • ⚠️ Registration docs are in the post and the justificante expires 8 July — chase the gestoría NOW; no original docs = no Belarus crossing.
  • ⚠️ Get the 80–90 km/h vibration fixed at Aistren + written report — that is your cruising speed for ~6,000 km round trip.
  • Confirm each passport's Belarus entry rules (visa / visa-free) with the embassy.
  • Book the DGT appointment for the IDP (~€10).
  • Buy the Czech e-vignette timed to cover 18 July (and the return date if driving back).
  • Check tyre pressures loaded (2.3–2.5 bar front / 2.1–2.3 rear) — rear tyres are from 2023, inspect tread before departure.
